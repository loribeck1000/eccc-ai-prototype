Indigenous Data Governance

OCAP and CARE-compliant data governance frameworks

OCAP Principles

🌴

Ownership

First Nations communities own their cultural knowledge, data, and information collectively.

🔒

Control

Communities control all aspects of data management including collection, use, and disclosure.

🔑

Access

Communities have the right to access their own data and manage access by others.

🏠

Possession

Physical control of data through community-managed systems and infrastructure.

CARE Principles

🤝

Collective Benefit

Data ecosystems designed for Indigenous innovation and self-determination.

⚖

Authority to Control

Indigenous peoples' rights to govern data creation, collection, and use.

📜

Responsibility

Accountability for nurturing respectful relationships with Indigenous data.

♥

Ethics

Minimizing harm and maximizing benefit aligned with Indigenous values.

Governance Framework Implementation

1

Data Classification

Automated classification of environmental data based on Indigenous knowledge content, territorial boundaries, and community significance.

2

Consent Management

Granular consent workflows enabling communities to specify exactly how their data can be used, by whom, and for what purposes.

3

Access Control

Role-based and community-based access controls with full audit logging and sovereignty-preserving encryption.

4

Data Stewardship

Community-designated data stewards with authority to manage, modify, or revoke data access at any time.
